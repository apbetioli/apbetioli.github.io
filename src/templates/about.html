There and back again

A programmer tale by Alexandre Betioli

My first program was a quiz coded in a Windows 98 ".bat" file. It was created after long hours of exploring files on my computer, trying to understand how programs worked, and using a BASIC programming language book as a resource.

When I discovered the Internet, I began researching how to create my own video game. I started by making my own CounterStrike map, then moved to programming a first-person shooter game in which the gun was a fish that shot fireworks. I found my passion in programming.

My first website was a photolog written in PHP and MySQL, which I created to share photos with my family. All family members could log in and post their photos. Facebook wasn't a thing yet.

After graduating, I had the opportunity to work with diverse tech stacks, teams, and industries.

When I worked at Objective, a medium-sized company with 100-200 developers, I found an opportunity to specialize in the front end, given that 99% of those developers were backend/desktop Java developers. Being a front-end developer allowed me to work on the newest company projects and with international customers, including Google.

Outside of work, I always wanted to build something myself. So, I involved my friend Thiago in my craziest ideas, which led us to be finalists at the 2013 Google Cloud Developer Contest and earn a Nexus7 tablet. We also started a 3D printing e-commerce, building our printers from scratch (this one deserves its own post). Later, we tried building video games and created a Unity asset with 200+ sales in the Unity Asset Store.

My son was born then, and I saw myself needing more time to continue with my outside work ideas. I sold my house, and I started my own company full-time with the house money. After studying marketing, I wanted to try selling online courses, so I started with a YouTube channel on 3d printing and created a 3d printing course. But it was too much work for me alone, creating content, editing videos, and doing marketing. So I convinced Mariana to join my venture, where she would create content and the courses, and I would focus on the tech and marketing. We sold 1500+ course subscriptions and created a platform for users to watch the courses. We've survived for almost three years until we ran out of money. I also missed being a full-time programmer.

For the past two years, I've been working remotely for an AI company based in Berlin, creating tools for AI developers in the NLP field. It's an exciting time to work with ML and AI.
